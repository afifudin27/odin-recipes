Soto

Soto is a traditional Indonesian soup mainly composed of broth, meat, and vegetables. Soto is sometimes considered Indonesia's national dish, as it is served form Sumatra to Papua, in wide range of variations. Soto is omnipresent in Indonesia, available in many warungs (small eatery or cafe) and open-air eateries on many streets corners, to fine dining restaurants and luxurious hotels.

Ingredients

For the main dish

  • chicken (sliced)
  • 2 liters of water
  • 2cm ginger (crushed)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5 lime leaves
  • 2 lemongrass stalks (crushed)
  • 4cm galangal
  • salt and chicken stok powder

For the fine seasoning

  • 8 cloves of onions
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 5cm tumeric
  • 2 grains of candlenut
  • 1/2 teaspoon of peppercorns

For the complementary

  • Soun (brewed with hot water, drained)
  • Bean sprouts (brewed with hot water, drained)
  • Potato cakes
  • Boiled eggs
  • Sambal (spicy sauce)
  • Celery leeks (thinly sliced)
  • Fried onions
  • Koya powder

Steps

  1. Prepare all the ingredients and kitchen utensils needed.
  2. Boil chicken with ginger, cook until boiling and remove the foam.
  3. Saute all the ground spices (fine seasoning) until you can smell the aroma.
  4. Add bay leaves, lime leaves, galangal, and lemongrass. Saute until the leaves wilt and the other spices are cooked.
  5. Pour the stir-fried spices into the chicken cooking water, and cook until the chicken is cooked.
  6. Remove the chicken and drain, and set aside the gravy.
  7. Fry the chicken until slightly dry, then shred according to taste.
  8. For the koya powder, you can use instant or make your own by mashing prawn crackers and fried garlic.
  9. Arrange the soun and bean sprouts in a bowl, pour the soto sauce, add the complementary ingredients.
  10. Serve and enjoy the typical Chicken Soto!
