Sate

Sate or satay is a Southeast Asian dish of seasoned, skewered, and grilled meat, served with sauce. The earliest preparations of sate is believed to have originated in Javanese cuisine, but has spread to almost anywhere in Indonesia, where it has become a national dish. Sate is often served with Peanut sauce (a sauce made from Peanut butter) and is often accompanied with lontong (a type of rice cake).

Ingredients

  • Chicken meat (cut into little strips)
  • 200 grams of fried peanuts
  • 5 cloves of onion
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 2 pieces of candlenut, split in half
  • 4 pieces of curly red chili
  • 70 ml of coconut milk
  • 170 ml of water
  • sugar to taste
  • salt to taste
  • cooking oil to taste

Steps

  1. Blend fried peanuts, candlenut, curly red chili, garlic, onion, and water.
  2. Heat oil in a frying pan, pour in the peanut sauce, and saute until oily.
  3. Add the coconut milk, and stir again a few moments.
  4. Add sugar and salt to taste. Stir until the texture starts to get thick, but don't let it dry out.
  5. Take the chicken that has been cut into small pieces, then attach it to a skewer (3-4 meats on one skewer).
  6. Grill the skewered chicken over hot charcoal.
  7. Once cooked, move the chicken and pour the peanut sauce on it.
